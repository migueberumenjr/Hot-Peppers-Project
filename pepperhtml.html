Comparing the Spiciness of 45 Hot Peppers: The Scoville Heat Scale

There are over 400 peppers in the world. Numerical spiciness scores are assigned on the basis of the pungency of the peppers, which is measured in Scoville Heat Units (SHU), a function of the capsaicin concentration in a pepper. Most of the spiciest peppers tend to be red and brown in color, and are grown in North America the Caribbean, particularly in the US and Trinidad. The pepper species Chinense dominates the rest of the hottest peppers. It's incredible how spicy, numerically, peppers can get!
